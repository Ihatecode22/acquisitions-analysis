Traditionally the focus is on acquisition spend

Acquisition spend in aggregate has increased over the last 10 years

When evaluating acquisitions whether at investment banks or in the media, the norm is to focus on aggregate spend.

The chart shows that the Big 5 tech companies have continued to increase acquisition spend in aggregate over the last 10 years. The expectation is for this trend to continue.

Unfortunately, this chart can only provide limited granularity since only the biggest transactions have specific transaction data.


However, what's more interesting to investigate is not how much money is spent, but rather what the money is spent on

Interesting findings across AI/ML/Analytics, AR/VR, and Security

Trends in AI

There has been a steady increase in the acquisitions of companies working in the field of artificial intelligence and machine learning. Google is the most dominant of the Big 5 with 45 of the 99 acquisitions since 2000.

Trends in AR/VR

Given the hype regarding AR/VR, there have been surprisingly few acquisitions in this category. Is it because it is a fairly new field? Facebook is leading this space with OculusVR's acquisition but Google caught up with 3 acquisitons of its own

Trends in Security

It is surprising to see the lack of acquisitions in the security domain . Given the importance of security today, it could be that most companies look at security as an integral component of their product and hence work on it internally.

Other interesting insights across acquisition categories

AI/ML/Analytics AR/VR Hardware Software Media/Commerce/UserDB Security

Artificial Intelligence / Machine Learning / Analytics

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning seem to be the technology of the future.

None of the big five want to be left behind. Similar to Software companies, most AI/ML/Analytics companies were acquired when they were 2-5 years old and have a head count < 100 employees.

Google and Microsoft have acquired more than 2/3rds of all companies.




Augmented Reality / Virtual Reality

This is one of the newest technologies and hence not a lot of companies working on AR/VR have been acquired till now.

Oculus VR was the one big name acquisition by Facebook in this space.

Amazon has not yet entered this space but the remaining 4 are almost at par with the number of companines acquired in this category.




Hardware

2012-14 was a peak period for Hardware companies to get acquired.

The major acquisitions in this category are Nest and Motorola Mobility by Google and Nokia by Microsoft.

Apple also made its biggest acquisition (by spend), Beats, in this category.

Interestingly, the average age of Hardware companies when acquired is the highest (10.8 years) across all categories.



Media / Commerce / User Databases

Amazon leads this category with 24 out of 66 companies acquired mainly due to its interest in expanding its commerce presence.

Unlike the other categories like AI/ML/Analytics and Software, the big acquisitions (LinkedIn, Whole Foods, Souq.com) are > 10 years old when acquired and have a large headcount (>5,000 employees)





Security

The few companies that are acquired in this domain are dominated by Google and Microsoft.

reCAPTCHA is the only large security company (>5,000 employees) that has been acquired (it was acquired by Google).






Software

Majority of the software companies are acquired between the ages of 2-5 years and more than 85% of all software companies acquired have less than a 100 employees.

This category also has the most number of acquisitons and this trend is still on the rise.
